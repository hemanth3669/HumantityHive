What is the Right to Education? 🎓

The Right to Education means everyone, no matter who they are, has the right to learn, grow, and build a better future through education. Schools, teachers, and learning opportunities are for everyone — kids and adults alike! 🌍

Story: Zara and the Big Tree School 🌳

In the village of Brightvale, Zara, a little girl, loved to learn but had no school to attend. She would sit under a big tree 🌳, drawing in the dirt with a stick. One day, a teacher named Mr. Rahul passed by and saw Zara.

“Why are you studying here?” he asked. “We don’t have a school,” she said sadly. Inspired, Mr. Rahul gathered the villagers. “Every child has the right to learn!” he said. Together, they built a school under the big tree. Soon, kids of all ages came to learn, share stories, and dream big. Zara became the happiest girl, saying, “Learning is for everyone!”

Moral of the Story ✨:

“Education is a right, not a privilege. Everyone deserves the chance to learn and build a brighter future.” 🌈
