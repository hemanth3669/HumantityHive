What is the Right to Equality? 🤝

The Right to Equality means that everyone is treated the same, no matter their race, gender, religion, or background. Non-Discrimination ensures no one is treated unfairly because they are different. Together, these rights make the world a better and kinder place! 🌍✨

Story: The Rainbow Parade 🎉

In the town of Harmonyville, the animals decided to hold a big parade 🥳. The elephants 🐘 said, “We’ll lead because we’re the biggest!” but the sparrows 🐦 chirped, “That’s not fair! We’re small but just as important.”

Then, wise old Tilly the Turtle 🐢 spoke up: “In a rainbow 🌈, every color is different, but together, they’re beautiful. Our parade should be the same—everyone is equal!”

So, they marched side by side—big and small, furry and feathered—proving that everyone, no matter how different, deserves the same respect and place in the world.

Moral of the Story ✨:

"We are all unique, but everyone deserves equal treatment and respect. Differences make the world beautiful." 🌍✨
