What is Freedom of Religion?

The Freedom of Religion means everyone has the right to believe in and practice any religion—or none at all—without fear or discrimination. It ensures respect for everyone’s beliefs.

Story: The Festival of Lanterns 🏮

In the town of Sunridge, people from different faiths lived together—some prayed in temples 🛕, others in mosques 🕌, churches ⛪, or under the open sky 🌌. One day, the children decided to hold a big lantern festival 🏮.

Each child painted their lantern with symbols from their traditions. When night fell, they lit the lanterns and released them into the sky 🌟.

Everyone watched in awe as the lanterns floated together, blending their colors and light. “Even though we believe in different things, we’re all part of the same sky,” said Zara, a little girl.

From that day, the lantern festival became a tradition to celebrate unity in diversity.

Moral of the Story:

"Everyone has the right to follow their beliefs, and respecting each other’s differences brings us closer together." 🕊️✨
